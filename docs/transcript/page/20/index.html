Media and the criminal courts in Scotland

Blogging and microblogging in court. James Doleman started blogging during the Tommy Sheridan trial in 2006.

 Published on November 1, 2012  |  34 minutes  |  7033 words  |  Iriss

IW - Ian Watson JD - James Doleman James Doleman started blogging during the Tommy Sheridan trial in 2006. At a seminar organised by Strathclyde University’s Centre for Law, Crime and Justice, James talked about why social media and criminal courts should be seen as an opportunity rather than a problem. The vast majority of court proceedings are not reported at all, and a great many are under-reported. Social media offers us a new set of media tools that can open up justice and enable citizens to take an active role in the world, going to court rooms or local Council meetings and telling people what is happening.
